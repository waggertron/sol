# Source Card Standards

Use this format for source cards in `kb/cards/`.

## Required Sections

- Citation
- Source registry id
- Domain
- Key contribution
- Model implications
- Limits and cautions
- Evidence grade
- Useful search terms

## Evidence Grades

- `A`: meta-analysis, major review, foundational paper, established framework,
  or replicated body of evidence
- `B`: peer-reviewed empirical paper with relevant methods and limits
- `C`: credible framework, official documentation, or professional reference
  that is not direct evidence for the product claim
- `D`: speculative, indirect, or internal hypothesis

## Writing Rules

- Summarize in project-relevant terms.
- Avoid long quotations.
- Distinguish what the source shows from what the product might infer.
- Identify limits, population/context constraints, and misuse risks.
- State whether the source changes the ontology, signal matrix, generation
  mapping, safety boundary, or research backlog.
- Prefer conservative language for personality inference.

## Model Implication Questions

Ask:

- Does this source justify a new construct?
- Does it strengthen or weaken an existing construct?
- Does it change what data may support an inference?
- Does it affect confidence, context, recency, or user confirmation rules?
- Does it introduce a blocked or high-sensitivity category?
- Does it help map profile atoms to generation controls?
