## Key Contribution

ICD-11 moved personality disorder classification toward a dimensional model that
emphasizes severity and trait qualifiers. This is relevant because maladaptive
personality extremes overlap linguistically with everyday trait language, but
they carry clinical meaning only in appropriate assessment contexts.

## Model Implications

The product should not diagnose personality disorders. It can still learn from
dimensional clinical frameworks to avoid simplistic categories and to understand
why severity, impairment, distress, and context matter.

Clinical trait language should be blocked or heavily constrained in user-facing
outputs unless the project becomes a regulated clinical product.

## Limits and Cautions

Clinical frameworks are not personalization schemas. They should mainly inform
risk boundaries and language safety.
