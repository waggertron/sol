## Key Contribution

The Big Five is a broad descriptive taxonomy for personality traits. It is
especially useful as a shared language for high-level trait tendencies:

- openness to experience
- conscientiousness
- extraversion
- agreeableness
- neuroticism or emotional stability

For this project, the Big Five should be treated as a broad organizing layer,
not as a complete representation of a person.

## Model Implications

The knowledge model should use broad traits as parent categories for narrower
facets and observable indicators. Broad trait scores should rarely map directly
to generation controls. Facets and observed behavior are usually better controls.

Example:

- broad trait: openness
- possible facets: aesthetic interest, intellectual curiosity, novelty seeking
- generation controls: novelty level, abstraction, genre variety, visual
  complexity

## Limits and Cautions

Broad trait models are useful summaries, but they can hide within-person
variability, context dependence, cultural limits, and measurement uncertainty.
