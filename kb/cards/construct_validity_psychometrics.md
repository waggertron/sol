# Source Card: Construct Validity

## Citation

Cronbach, L. J., and Meehl, P. E. (1955). Construct validity in psychological
tests. Psychological Bulletin, 52(4), 281-302. https://doi.org/10.1037/h0040957

Source registry id: `cronbach_meehl_1955_construct_validity`

## Domain

- psychometrics
- measurement theory
- construct validity

## Key Contribution

Construct validity asks whether a measurement actually captures the theoretical
construct it claims to measure. For this project, it is the guardrail against
turning weak cues into confident-sounding personality labels.

## Model Implications

Every profile construct should have a nomological role:

- what it means
- what evidence should support it
- what evidence should not support it
- related constructs it should correlate with
- constructs it should remain distinct from
- expected behavioral or generation outcomes

Profile atoms should include evidence, counterevidence, source modality,
confidence, context, and user confirmation status.

## Limits and Cautions

Construct validity is not solved once. It is an ongoing argument supported by
evidence. Product metrics such as "users liked the output" do not automatically
validate the personality construct.
