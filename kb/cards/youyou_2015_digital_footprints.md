# Source Card: Computer-Based Personality Judgments

## Citation

Youyou, Kosinski, and Stillwell (2015). Computer-based personality judgments are
more accurate than those made by humans. PNAS.
https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25583507/

Source registry id: `youyou_2015_computer_personality_judgments`

## Domain

- digital footprints
- personality prediction
- computational social science
- human-computer judgment comparison

## Key Contribution

This study is a central example showing that digital behavior can predict
self-reported personality well enough to compete with human judgments under
specific data and evaluation conditions.

## Model Implications

Digital traces can carry meaningful signal, but the project should separate:

- predictive accuracy under a research benchmark
- user-facing interpretability
- consent and acceptable use
- generation utility

The profile should include provenance and confidence rather than silently
converting behavioral traces into personality labels.

## Limits and Cautions

This line of work is also a warning. If digital traces can predict private
attributes, the product must avoid covert profiling and must build deletion,
inspection, consent, and sensitive-inference gates from the start.
