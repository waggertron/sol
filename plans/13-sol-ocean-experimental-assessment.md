# Experimental Sol OCEAN Assessment Plan

## Purpose

Execute Phase 3 of `plans/08-ocean-assessment-plan.md` without presenting an
original Sol instrument as validated.

Reusable agent workflow:

- `.codex/skills/sol-experimental-assessment-workflow/SKILL.md`

## Safety And Evidence Posture

- Candidate items are design artifacts, not a personality diagnosis or ground
  truth measure.
- The candidate remains outside `assessments/ocean/manifest.json` until pilot
  and validation gates pass.
- Existing instruments may inform construct coverage, but candidate wording
  must be original and checked for exact collisions.
- Broad OCEAN scores remain provisional self-report evidence and do not become
  direct global generation controls.
- No reliability, validity, norm, invariance, or predictive claim is allowed
  before empirical evidence exists.

## Phase B: Item-Writing Contract

- [x] Define response scale, item metadata, keying balance, reading target,
  sensitivity labels, and expected failure modes.
- [x] Require two original candidate items per subconstruct: one positive-keyed
  and one negative-keyed.
- [x] Keep clinical, competence, morality, protected-trait, and eligibility
  language out of the pool.

## Phase C: Candidate Form

- [x] Draft `Sol-OCEAN-Quick-v0` as a 30-item design-review candidate.
- [x] Keep the form outside the administrable manifest.
- [x] Add structural validation and exact collision checks against stored
  instruments.

## Phase D: Expert And Cognitive Review

- [ ] Conduct independent construct review by a qualified psychometrician.
- [ ] Review wording for ambiguity, reading burden, cultural loading, social
  desirability, and acquiescence effects.
- [ ] Run cognitive interviews with consenting participants.
- [ ] Revise or remove items; freeze a named pilot form only after review.

## Phase E: Empirical Pilot

- [ ] Pre-register pilot questions, exclusions, scoring, and analysis.
- [ ] Collect a sufficiently justified consenting sample.
- [ ] Estimate item distributions, missingness, internal consistency, factor
  structure, and test-retest reliability.
- [ ] Evaluate convergent/discriminant relationships against a permissive
  benchmark and inspect subgroup/measurement-invariance risks.
- [ ] Publish limitations and decide whether to revise, abandon, or advance.

## Activation Gate

The candidate must not enter `assessments/ocean/manifest.json` or emit product
profile atoms until Phase D review is complete and an explicit ADR approves a
limited pilot. Product use requires later empirical evidence from Phase E.
